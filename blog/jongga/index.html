Emblem and Branding Design for Korean Traditional Houses, Jongga

June 30th, 2015

Jongga refers to a Korean family and house living from generation to generation. Jonggas in Gyeongsangbuk-do, they stand for a 500-year history of Korean culture and as a support for the identity of Gyeongsangbuk-do's culture.

Under a centralized dynasty system in traditional Korea, it was difficult for general people to independently create and use the emblem of the family when they are not royal family. Therefore, unlike the West, Jongga has continued its tradition based on the central house without their emblems or symbols except their house's signboard.

CHALLENGE

As the modernization progressed, many people left their hometown and moved to the city. Therefore, the tradition and cultural meaning of the Jongga began to fade. To address these problems and to protect their cultural heritage and spirit, the Gyeongsangbuk-do Jongga project was started. We developed emblems for Jonggas to solidify the traditional value through visualization. In 2nd and 3rd projects, we created design applications and products based on their emblems.

We aim to preserve and transmit cultural value of the nation by branding the "spirit and heritage” of Jongga.

We visited Jonggas in Gyeongsangbuk-do province in the order indicated on the map above to collect data and stories related to their houses and families. With the collected data and the legends and stories behind each houses, we were able to create a unique emblem for each Jongga.

Two bar design, which is applied commonly to all emblems, symbolizes the open door of the Jongga. In times of need, they opened barns to share foods with general people or peasantry. We intended to show and inherit the spirit of the Jongga.
